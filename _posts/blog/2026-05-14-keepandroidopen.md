Yes, the topic of Google blocking every apps from unregistered developers is
already in the world for some days and I was simply ignoring it. Truth said: I'm
too tired (and too old) to jump through Google's kafkaesque hoops and loops just
to publish a little hobby app. Whenever some distant country in the Himalaya
creates a law to protect pink three legged cats, I have to provide to Google a
detailed list of all my three-legged cats, their medical records and my offical
three-legged-cat-holder-license. On deadline. Or my dev account will be
canceled.

>   Catch-22 side note: When I tried to remove my Google dev account myself, I
>   was stopped, because somewhere exists a single copy of my only app. Probably
>   on one of the old phones rotting in some box on the attic. Since then I
>   ignore every requirement from Google until they close my account. 🤷‍♀️

So, when the topic first arose, I shrugged and put it on the stack with all the
unanswered "Google Developer Policy Changes require you to..." mails. It only
came back to my attention when I recently opened F-Droid and saw
[keepandroidopen.org][kao]-banner. I promptly I +1'ed the [change.org
petition][pet] and added the website banner. And since the only one reading this
blog is probably Google's search bot, it may even reach the right receiver. Who
probably will ignore it as always since they shredded "don't be evil".

[kao]: https://keepandroidopen.org/  
[pet]: https://www.change.org/p/stop-google-from-limiting-apk-file-usage/
